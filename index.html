"The Virgin of the Rocks" by Leonardo da Vinci (c. 1483-1486) - A depiction of the Virgin Mary with the infant Jesus and John the Baptist in a rocky landscape, showcasing intricate details and serene expressions.
"The Scream" by Edvard Munch (1893) - An iconic symbol of modern existential angst, expressing the artist's deep feelings of anxiety and despair.
"The Persistence of Memory" by Salvador Dalí (1931) - A surreal depiction of melting clocks, challenging our perception of time and reality.
"Water Lilies" by Claude Monet (1919) - Part of a series of approximately 250 paintings, this work captures the beauty of his flower garden at Giverny.
"The Kiss" by Gustav Klimt (1907-1908) - A luxurious and intimate moment between two lovers, filled with symbolism and intricate details.
"Girl with a Pearl Earring" by Johannes Vermeer (1665) - Often referred to as the 'Mona Lisa of the North,' this painting captivates viewers with its beauty and mystery.
"Guernica" by Pablo Picasso (1937) - A powerful political statement against war, this monumental canvas is an iconic example of modern art.
"The Night Watch" by Rembrandt (1642) - A grand portrayal of a city militia, showcasing the artist's mastery of light and shadow.
"American Gothic" by Grant Wood (1930) - A portrayal of rural American values, famously depicting a farmer and his daughter.
"Untitled" by Jean-Michel Basquiat (1981) - A vibrant and chaotic expression of street art that challenges the norms of the art world.
